# Typography

## Goals
- Establish obvious hierarchy in under two seconds.
- Pair one expressive display face with one dependable reading face.
- Use fluid sizing with `clamp()` so the design scales instead of snapping.

## Guidelines
- Prefer a strong point-of-view over generic defaults.
- Use weight, scale, tracking, and case intentionally; do not rely on color alone.
- Keep body copy easy to scan with stable line length and consistent rhythm.
- Reserve monospace for code, data, and technical instrumentation rather than as a vibe shortcut.

## Avoid
- Interchangeable system-only stacks by default.
- Decorative heading fonts with poor numerals or unreadable small sizes.
- Five unrelated font families in one surface.
